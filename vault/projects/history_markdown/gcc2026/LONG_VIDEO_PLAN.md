## Audience

People who land on jmchilton.net or a paper page, who want the full reproducibility-in-age-of-agents argument and don't have a conference seat. Expected duration: **12–18 minutes** (vs. 10 for the talk).

## Goals (where this differs from the talk)

1. **Develop the thesis fully** — 4–6 minutes on the "automating the reproducibility crisis" argument with examples, counterexamples, and the specific Galaxy claim. The talk gets 2 minutes; this gets the room.
2. **Deeper Foundry segment** — pull in the *compiler* framing from `vault/papers/foundry/index.md` (Patterns / Molds / Pipelines / Schemas / Casts / provenance). The talk only gestures at this.
3. **Reuse the 3-minute case-study clips verbatim** for demo 1 and demo 2 where useful — *or* film extended cuts (5–7 min each) if the short cuts feel insufficient.
4. **End with the three papers** as concrete next reading.
